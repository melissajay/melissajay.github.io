About

Thanks for visiting my website! My name is Melissa Jay, and I am a fourth-year PhD Candidate in Biostatistics at the University of Iowa. I am working with Dr. Jacob Oleson on my dissertation research, which focuses on statistical methods for spatio-temporal modeling of cancer risk. We are developing methods for addressing zero inflation in the estimation of age-adjusted rates and methods for performing Bayesian mediation analyses with spatial datasets. I am also broadly interested in Bayesian statistics, survival analysis, and cancer epidemiology applications. My research is supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from Colorado College in 2016. As an undergraduate, I participated in numerous research experiences to determine how I wanted to apply math to solve real world problems. I participated in the Iowa Summer Institute in Biostatistics (where I discovered my love for biostatistics), Research in Industrial Projects for Students - Hong Kong, and a senior thesis project modeling waiting list dynamics under different kidney transplant allocation policies. I was awarded the Barry Goldwater Scholarship for my dedication to a research career and my potential for making significant contributions to scientific research.

Prior to graduate school, I worked as a Junior Statistician and Technical Writing Lead at Dascena. In this role, I contributed to the development of machine learning algorithms to predict sepsis onset and patient decompensation in the hospital using Electronic Health Record (EHR) data. I built and managed databases, performed data analyses, and drafted scientific manuscripts.

In my free time, I enjoy rock climbing, hiking, and spending time with my husband, Brendan, and our pets, Arabelle (dog) and Chewbecky (rabbit).
